Why a Stand Mixer Is Worth It

A quality stand mixer frees your hands, handles heavy doughs that would burn out a hand mixer, and opens up recipes you'd otherwise skip. If you bake more than occasionally, it pays for itself in time and results within the first year.

What to Look For

  • Bowl size: 5 quarts handles most home recipes. Go bigger if you regularly make large batches.
  • Motor wattage: 300W+ for light use, 500W+ for bread dough and heavy mixing.
  • Attachments: If you want to expand beyond mixing, check the attachment ecosystem before buying.
  • Warranty: 1 year is the minimum. 3 years is better. Lifetime warranties exist on some commercial models.
  • Noise: All stand mixers make noise. If your kitchen is open-plan, check reviews for noise level at high speeds.
