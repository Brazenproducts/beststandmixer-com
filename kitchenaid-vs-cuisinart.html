Quick Verdict

The KitchenAid Artisan wins on brand reputation, attachment ecosystem, and long-term durability. The Cuisinart SM-50 wins on value — you get comparable performance for significantly less money. If budget isn't a concern, get the KitchenAid. If you want the best bang for your buck, the Cuisinart is hard to beat.

Which Should You Buy?

Buy the KitchenAid Artisan if: You bake regularly, want access to the full attachment ecosystem (pasta maker, meat grinder, ice cream maker, etc.), and plan to keep the machine for 10+ years. The brand support and parts availability are unmatched.

Buy the Cuisinart SM-50 if: You want solid performance without the premium price tag. The 3-year warranty is actually better than KitchenAid's 1-year, and the extra bowl capacity is useful for larger batches.
